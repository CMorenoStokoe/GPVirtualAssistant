Appointment types

​Click the buttons below to explore our different appointment types with our doctors:

Pre-bookable

These appointments can be booked up to six weeks in advance. Limited numbers of appointments are available with each of the GPs therefore giving you an opportunity to book with a GP of your choice.

On the Day Appointments

Each GP has a limited number of same day appointments which go very quickly. We are offering the maximum number of appointments we can. If there is a high demand on the day and you consider your case to be urgent you will be contacted by the on call GP and offered an appointment if necessary.
On the day appointments should really be used for problems that cannot wait. They should not be used for routine consultations.

Telephone Consultations

We offer telephone consultations with a GP on a daily basis, this is usually between the times of 11.30 to 6.00pm

Short Notice Waiting Lists

The GPs do have “waiting lists” which you can ask to be put on in case an appointment becomes free. You will be contacted by the reception staff and offered an appointment so it is very important that we have the correct contact number for you.

Home Visits

If you are too ill to come to the practice you (or a relative/carer) may request a home visit. Please try to do this before 10.30am. The GPs may contact you by telephone before visiting in order to be prepared for the visit.

Other Clinics and Services

​ Community Nursing Team (District Nurses): Happy to advise on the nursing care of the elderly, disabled and those recently discharged.

The Health Visitors Team: Dedicated to health promotion and disease prevention advice, particularly to parents and children under 5. They also run a 'drop in' child welfare & baby weighing clinic at Elmbridge Childrens Centre, Windfall Way, Elmbridge, Gloucester on Wednesdays between 1:30 and 2:30pm. ​

​Mental Health Nurse: Holds a counselling session at the surgery on a weekly basis. Appointments for this service are only made by referral from the doctors.

Community Midwife: Looks after our patients throughout their pregnancy and arrange their own clinics at our surgery. They also run parent craft classes in association with the health visitors.

Travel advice: Our nurses can advise on travel vaccinations and precautions.
